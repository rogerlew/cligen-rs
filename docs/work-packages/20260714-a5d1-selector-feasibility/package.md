# A5d1 Development-Library Selector Feasibility

Status: `EXECUTED-HOLD-PATH-INFEASIBILITY`
Date: 2026-07-14
Evidence mode: Mixed (ran/static/derived)

## Objective

Resolve the primary A5d0 contract hold by determining whether one
deterministic, bounded complete-year selector can improve the complete
registered interannual feasibility vector at all 17 exposed development
stations while satisfying the monthly/daily preservation, calendar, reuse,
common-prefix, numerical, and physical-row invariants. A successful package
establishes only `STRUCTURALLY-FEASIBLE`; it does not authorize confirmation,
freeze a public candidate identifier, or satisfy the independent A5d2
evaluation and A5d3 corpus requirements.

## Decision boundary

A5d1 is a development proof, not a candidate campaign. Fourier/EOF features
describe complete-year state and may guide weights or transitions, but they
may not multiply, add to, clip, repair, or otherwise mutate daily physical
fields. The base library is generated by the accepted
`faithful_5_32_3` profile with `qc_filter: off`. A selector may change which
complete block supplies a target year and may relabel date/index bookkeeping;
the selected precipitation, temperature, radiation, wind, dewpoint, and storm
payloads must remain bitwise identical to their source block.

The package must separate two questions:

1. **Marginal feasibility:** do nonnegative constrained block weights
   reallocate within-year variance toward the complete annual target vector
   while preserving the registered monthly/daily surface?
2. **Path feasibility:** can a bounded calendar-compatible path realize the
   required 30-/100-year dependence and prefix behavior without violating the
   frozen exact-block reuse and cross-boundary rules?

A pass on either question alone is not a structural pass.

## Scope

Included:

- hash-lock the A5d0 decision and closure evidence, ADR-0004, the accepted A5a
  station/corpus identities, the A5b development coefficient inputs, every
  reused tool, and the exact Rust binary used to build libraries;
- use exactly the 17 already exposed A5a/A5b stations as development evidence;
- regenerate deterministic complete-year libraries from
  `faithful_5_32_3 + qc_filter: off`, rather than treating A5b's retained
  shared-base archive as if it contained the missing daily trajectories;
- freeze an ordered pool-size grid, burn/seed identities, cost ceilings, and a
  deterministic escalation/selection rule before actual-library solver or
  path outcomes are inspected;
- derive day- and month-count-weighted block sufficient statistics, including
  the registered precipitation, Tmax, Tmin, occurrence, transition,
  cross-variable, and storm/descriptor preservation features;
- freeze the complete feasibility vector, constraint formulas, units,
  normalization, tolerances, objective ordering, solver/version, tie rules,
  iteration limits, and infeasibility-certificate behavior before solving;
- solve stationary constrained weights separately from finite path ordering
  and emit independently replayable feasibility or infeasibility certificates;
- compare only predeclared bounded path alternatives: replacement with an
  exact reuse guard, weighted permutation/depletion, and state-persistent
  conditional selection of a different compatible block;
- construct each 100-year development path once and define its 30-year result
  strictly as the first 30 years of that same path;
- test 365-/366-day compatibility, date relabeling, non-immediate reuse,
  cooldown, cross-year wet/dry transitions and spells, deterministic failure,
  runtime, and memory; and
- issue a reviewed cross-station `STRUCTURALLY-FEASIBLE` decision or an honest
  named hold with the first corrective action.

Excluded:

- acquiring, qualifying, building, opening, fitting, or scoring any A5d
  confirmation or independent-sensitivity target;
- using development effect sizes or selected paths later as confirmation
  evidence;
- running WEPP or calibrating/revising climate gates, bootstrap availability,
  null false-failure, or a downstream response rule (A5d2 owns those tasks);
- choosing or sealing a confirmation corpus (A5d3 owns that task);
- additive or multiplicative daily overlays, physical-value intervention, or
  substitution of another model family after results are inspected;
- changing faithful generation, legacy `.par`, accepted station documents,
  runspecs, generation-profile enums/defaults, provenance, typed output, or
  `SPEC-A5-EVALUATION` revision 3;
- freezing a candidate, station-model, coefficient-grammar, or public profile
  identifier; and
- adding or changing production functions under `crates/`. Research-only
  feasibility tooling belongs under this package's `artifacts/` tree.

## Development and exposure lock

Before any actual-library optimization result is opened, execution must emit
a strict machine-readable input lock and an exposure ledger. The allowlist is
limited to accepted authorities, existing public generator inputs, the 17
exposed station/Daymet development records, explicitly named A5b research
inputs, regenerated faithful-off libraries, and synthetic fixtures created in
this package.

The lock must record source commit, tool and dependency identities, station
and period roles, raw/normalized hashes where applicable, exact generator
controls, pool seeds/burns, calendar policy, and an isolated hash-bound A5d1
input snapshot containing no confirmation object. Existing A5a/A5b Daymet and
GHCN values remain development evidence even if this package does not need all
of them. If A5d3 creates sealed confirmation objects elsewhere, their
existence does not invalidate A5d1; A5d1 tooling may record path-level
exclusions but must never traverse or open those objects. No relabeling can
make an exposed station confirmatory.

## Plan

### Phase 1 — Input, exposure, and compatibility lock

1. Reproduce the A5d0 and A5c verifiers and hash their accepted decision
   records.
2. Enumerate the 17 development stations and verify their station-parameter,
   Daymet-fit, and reused Fourier/EOF identities.
3. Record a fail-closed allowlist and zero-confirmation exposure ledger.
4. Lock the accepted generator binary, runspec/profile/QC controls, schemas,
   host/toolchain details, and output identity rules.
5. Prove that no accepted public surface or production source changes in this
   package.

### Phase 2 — Pre-result feasibility contract

Before opening actual-library solver/path results:

1. Freeze a versioned selector-feasibility contract and strict JSON Schema.
2. Define the full block feature vector and count weighting. At minimum it
   covers monthly first/second moments for precipitation/Tmax/Tmin, wet-day
   occurrence and transitions, precipitation-amount distribution support,
   cross-variable structure, temperature ordering, storm descriptors, and
   cross-block spell/transition terms.
3. Define a marginal annual development subvector covering annual
   precipitation and temperature dispersion and cross-variable covariance.
   Define a separate path/dependence subvector covering lag dependence and
   low-frequency behavior. The complete structural vector is the registered
   combination of both, but stationary weights are evaluated only against the
   marginal subvector and paths only against the dependence subvector.
4. Pin hard preservation equations/inequalities, feasibility-only
   noninferiority rules, strict-improvement rules, units, tolerances, and the
   deterministic objective hierarchy. These are development feasibility rules,
   not A5d2 promotion gates.
5. Freeze the authority for every constraint: fitted monthly quantities map
   to the accepted `.par`/station-model surface; unparameterized daily, storm,
   descriptor, and cross-boundary preservation maps to the prospectively fixed
   uniform faithful-off library reference; annual improvement maps to the
   exposed Daymet development target. Reference switching after library
   outcomes are inspected is forbidden.
6. Pin the solver and certificate type, numerical precision, dependency
   versions, pivot/tie behavior where controllable, independent residual
   replay, bounded iterations, and fail-closed treatment of ambiguous status.
7. Pin the ordered pool-size grid, resource ceilings, exact-block reuse and
   cooldown bounds, path alternatives, calendar policy, seed domains, and the
   deterministic rule for selecting at most one common research contract.
   Pool sizes are nested prefixes of one canonical station library. The freeze
   classifies burn offsets as components of that library or as sensitivity
   realizations—never independent replicates—and forbids per-station
   pool/burn/path-seed selection. The contract, algorithm, tolerances,
   hyperparameters, and fitting/selection rule are global; station-specific
   Fourier/EOF coefficients, weights, and paths produced by that rule are
   permitted.
8. Freeze the exact station × nested-pool × library-realization × algorithm ×
   path-seed matrix and its expected unique-cell count. Every preregistered
   path seed must pass for the selected global contract unless the selector is
   explicitly seedless. Smaller exploratory pools may fail, but the globally
   selected pool and every registered realization/seed must pass all stations.
9. Create positive, infeasible, near-tolerance, calendar-exhaustion,
   reuse-exhaustion, and tampered-certificate fixtures before real solving.
10. Transition the preregistration to an immutable frozen state before any
    actual-library solver/path result is opened. Bind its SHA-256 identity into
    every library, feature, certificate, path, matrix-cell, and result record.

No station-specific tolerance, algorithm exception, or post-result contract
relaxation is permitted.

### Phase 3 — Development-library regeneration

1. Recover and verify the exact station parameter bytes used by the accepted
   A5a baseline.
2. Build the accepted Rust generator from the locked source and generate the
   frozen library grid twice for every station using
   `faithful_5_32_3 + qc_filter: off`.
3. Validate complete years, 365-/366-day classes, physical finiteness,
   quality/provenance sidecars, lexical member ordering, and byte-repeat
   identity.
4. Emit a canonical manifest binding every runspec, climate, provenance,
   quality report, extracted block, and source hash.
5. Keep large reproducible pools outside ordinary Git unless retention is
   necessary. Any retained binary/archive artifact that belongs in version
   control must be placed under Git LFS deliberately and pass `git lfs fsck`.

### Phase 4 — Marginal weight feasibility

1. Extract canonical year/month sufficient-statistic rows directly from the
   locked physical library rows.
2. Solve the frozen constrained-weight problem at every station and pool size.
3. Independently replay primal/dual, bound, or other solver-appropriate
   certificates; never treat a library API's success flag alone as proof.
4. Verify nonnegative normalized weights, day/month count handling, every
   hard preservation residual, the marginal annual subvector rule, effective
   support, and deterministic tie resolution.
5. Preserve infeasibility certificates and the first failed constraint for
   every rejected station/configuration.

### Phase 5 — Finite path feasibility

1. Exercise only the frozen path alternatives with deterministic bounded
   termination.
2. Build one 100-year path for every frozen station/configuration/library-
   realization/path-seed cell (or the sole seedless cell) and derive the
   30-year path solely by prefix.
3. Check calendar-class matching without imputation, selected-index lineage,
   exact and non-immediate block reuse, cooldown, finite-prefix constraint
   residuals, the path/dependence subvector, and cross-boundary wet/dry/spell
   behavior.
4. Prove every selected physical payload row equals its source-block row
   bitwise; report date/index bookkeeping separately.
5. Measure worst-case iterations, runtime, peak memory, pool storage, and all
   bounded-failure paths against the frozen resource ceilings.

### Phase 6 — Cross-station decision

1. Apply one predeclared algorithm, contract, tolerance set, hyperparameter
   set, and deterministic fitting/selection rule across all 17 stations; do
   not select a different algorithm, pool, burn/realization, path seed, or
   tolerance per station. Station-specific Fourier/EOF coefficients, fitted
   weights, and paths are expected outputs, not contract exceptions.
2. Close `STRUCTURALLY-FEASIBLE` only when that common contract passes every
   marginal and path obligation at every station and both horizons.
3. Recommend at most one research contract for later A5d4 consideration,
   without assigning any candidate/profile/station-model public identifier.
   Any later change to its basis, pooling, hyperparameters, fitting rule,
   solver semantics, or path contract invalidates the A5d1 proof and requires
   rerunning it.
4. Otherwise close on the most specific legitimate hold and name the first
   follow-on action. Do not substitute an unregistered model family.

### Phase 7 — Review and closure

1. Run independent accuracy/numerical, scientific-validity, and
   consistency/exposure reviews under the scientific authoring protocol.
2. Have the lead disposition findings and obtain bounded rechecks for every
   P1/P2 correction.
3. Emit a strict package verifier, machine decision, immutable closure
   manifest, gate record, and roadmap/catalog disposition.

## Execution result

The controlling v6 experiment froze 306 unique cells at
`dd38484dca7da633ed30854c16074fa66b2a7a7fdd53b05253277125cdd99d22`
after retaining and invalidating the v2–v5 development attempts. The v6
library was generated twice at all 17 stations with byte-identical climate,
quality, and provenance outputs. All 15 synthetic fixtures passed.

Stationary marginal weights passed at 13/17 stations for the 128-year pool and
17/17 for the 256-year pool. At pool 256, all exact annual-mean, leap-mass,
maximum-weight, fitted-monthly, unparameterized, component-noninferiority, and
aggregate centered-improvement rules passed.

No finite path passed the complete contract. At the marginally eligible
256-year pool, 151/153 station-seed-algorithm cells first failed the combined
30-/100-year finite-prefix marginal replay and two first failed dependence
noninferiority. All 306 cells passed calendar, reuse/cooldown, positive-weight
selection, exact physical-row identity, and physical common-prefix checks.
The aggregate January transition correction is included in path construction
and replay, not applied post hoc.

The terminal decision is therefore the scoped
`EXECUTED-HOLD-PATH-INFEASIBILITY`: none of the three frozen algorithms under
the frozen tolerances, seeds, iteration ceiling, 17 exposed stations, and one
burn-0 library realizes the full contract. This is not a proof that all
complete-block selectors are infeasible.

The first corrective action is A5d1b: diagnose the 30-/100-year empirical
block-multiset realization failure under the valid 256-year weights before
freezing another path algorithm. A5d2 and A5d3 remain independent required
packages; A5d4 remains blocked, and confirmation remains unauthorized.

## Execution & dispatch

Execution starts from current `origin/main` in repository
`rogerlew/cligen-rs`; the working and push target is `main`. No side branch is
created. Subtasks share the worktree, are read-only unless explicitly assigned
an artifact with non-overlapping ownership, and may not commit or push.

The package lead alone freezes contracts, opens actual-library results,
dispositions review findings, and publishes the terminal record. A kickoff
must repeat the no-confirmation boundary and verify a clean exposure ledger
before work begins.

## Exit criteria

### `EXECUTED-COMPLETE` with `STRUCTURALLY-FEASIBLE`

All of the following are required:

- one frozen common algorithm, contract, tolerance and hyperparameter set, and
  deterministic fitting/selection rule passes the full registered marginal
  and path contract at all 17 development stations and every required
  realization/path seed; station-specific fitted coefficients, weights, and
  paths are permitted outputs of that rule;
- the marginal annual subvector strictly improves under the frozen development
  rule without violating any monthly/daily preservation rule, and the separate
  path/dependence subvector passes its frozen improvement rule;
- both horizons come from one bounded 100-year path with exact 30-year
  selected-index and physical-row prefix identity plus the frozen semantic
  rules for horizon-dependent metadata;
- calendar, reuse, cross-boundary, deterministic replay, solver certificate,
  resource, and physical-row invariants all pass;
- no confirmation asset is accessed and no public compatibility surface
  changes; and
- independent review closes with no unresolved P1/P2 finding.

This outcome closes only A5d0's primary selector-feasibility hold. A5d2,
A5d3, and A5d4 remain mandatory, and A5d5 remains unauthorized.

### Legitimate holds

- `EXECUTED-HOLD-STRUCTURAL-INFEASIBILITY` — no frozen weight alternative can
  meet the complete marginal contract across all 17 stations;
- `EXECUTED-HOLD-PATH-INFEASIBILITY` — marginal weights exist, but no frozen
  bounded path alternative satisfies prefix, calendar, reuse, dependence, or
  cross-boundary rules across all stations;
- `EXECUTED-HOLD-LIBRARY-EVIDENCE` — the exact faithful-off daily libraries
  cannot be regenerated, validated, or hash-bound;
- `EXECUTED-HOLD-NUMERICAL-NONDETERMINISM` — certificates, tolerance behavior,
  solver ordering, or path replay cannot be made independently reproducible;
- `EXECUTED-HOLD-RESOURCE-BOUND` — every otherwise feasible registered
  alternative exceeds a prospectively frozen runtime, memory, or storage
  ceiling;
- `EXECUTED-HOLD-CONTRACT-INCOMPLETE` — required formulas, tolerances,
  calendar/reuse semantics, failure behavior, or evidence identities remain
  underspecified; and
- `EXECUTED-HOLD-EXPOSURE` — any confirmation candidate/target value is
  accessed before A5d4's evidence freeze.

Every hold records the failed criterion, evidence, and first corrective
action. None authorizes a different candidate family or confirmation run.
