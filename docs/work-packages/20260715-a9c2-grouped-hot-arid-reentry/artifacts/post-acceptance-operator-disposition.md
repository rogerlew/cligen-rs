## Operator decision

The two accepted development locations, AZ Yuma 27 ENE and CA Stovepipe
Wells 1 SW, are functionally adequate for continuation of the stochastic-
generator research campaign. The five-site floor is retired as a successor
entry requirement. No additional hot-arid station search, alternate
subhourly-network survey, or regime relabeling is required before returning to
the model comparison.

This is an explicit risk-acceptance and scope decision, not an empirical claim
that two stations provide the precision or external validity of five. The
operator accepts that users applying the eventual model in hot-arid settings
will need to interpret results with knowledge of local data limitations.

## Accepted limitations

- Hot-arid development evidence remains limited to two locations under the
  exact A8a descriptor screen.
- Each station receives equal total weight, but leave-one-site-out analysis
  reduces to a single-site contrast and cannot estimate broad spatial
  heterogeneity.
- Station and station-season results, event counts, uncertainty intervals, and
  sensitivity to either site's removal remain mandatory disclosures.
- Candidate-blind precision, false-failure, and power calculations remain
  useful diagnostics. Failure to reach a universal 0.80 power target does not
  by itself reopen the roster or stop the comparison; a nonfinite or undefined
  estimator remains a hard failure.
- The eventual evidence supports these development locations and registered
  strata only. It does not establish physical hot-arid universality, local
  calibration at every site, or production fitness.
- The locked 18-site confirmation roster and its target-series firewall are
  unchanged.

## Successor direction

The roadmap may now proceed to an A9c3 two-site grouped observed-development
comparison. That package should:

1. freeze the grouped objective and fit-side amendment before new candidate
   scoring;
2. retain Yuma and Stovepipe Wells without station substitution or new roster
   search;
3. preserve actual storm frequencies and eliminate the former uncalibrated
   per-station availability floors;
4. use equal station weighting and publish both pooled and site-specific
   diagnostics;
5. treat finite-sample precision/power as uncertainty evidence rather than a
   new corpus-construction gate;
6. refit and compare both registered candidate classes under a fresh campaign
   identity; and
7. state the two-site generalization limit in every candidate, selection, and
   downstream handoff artifact.

This disposition authorizes roadmap revision and future scaffolding only. It
does not authorize A9c3 execution, A9d confirmation access, A9e runtime work,
openWEPP integration, or WEPPcloud integration.
